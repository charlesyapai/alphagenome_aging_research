What this is

Two models are at work here:

  1. AlphaGenome (Google DeepMind, pretrained) predicts regulatory effects of a DNA variant across ~700 biosamples and 5+ output types (RNA-seq, ATAC, DNase, ChIP-histone, ChIP-TF).
  2. Aging classifier (this project, Random Forest on ~10k variants) takes those AlphaGenome scores and predicts whether the variant's regulatory signature looks like an aging-associated variant or a MAF-matched control.

Caveats: the aging label is heterogeneous (GWAS, longevity cohorts, aging-gene regions); controls are matched on population frequency but not trait-negative-verified; AlphaGenome predictions are model outputs, not direct measurements. Numbers are a useful prior, not a diagnosis.
